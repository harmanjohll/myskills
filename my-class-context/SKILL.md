# My Class Context

## The class

Primary 4 Robin. 30 students. Mixed readiness: three students working above level, five who need additional scaffolding. About one third of the class has English as a second language. Most students are confident verbally but struggle to write extended scientific explanations.

## Current topic

Photosynthesis. The idea that plants make their own food using light, water, and carbon dioxide.

## What they already know

- Plants have roots, stems, leaves, and flowers (Primary 3).
- Plants need water and sunlight to grow.
- Animals eat food to get energy; they have not yet been asked to compare this to how plants work.

## Misconceptions I am addressing

1. Plants get their food from the soil. Most students believe this firmly. The idea that plants manufacture food is counterintuitive to them.
2. Sunlight is food, not a raw material. Students say "plants eat sunlight" rather than understanding that sunlight is the energy source for a chemical process.
3. Photosynthesis and respiration are the same thing. Students who have heard both terms conflate them.

## Tone and language

Clear, curious, age-appropriate. Use Singapore examples where possible: the angsana trees along the road, the hibiscus, the monsoon drain with algae. Avoid idioms. Sentence-level scaffolding helps the ELL students.

## What I do not want

- Passive content. My students learn better when they have to make a decision or a prediction.
- Jargon introduced without definition. Chlorophyll and chloroplast can appear, but explain them immediately after using them.
- A quiz that only tests recall. I want something that surfaces understanding, not memorisation.

## What to do

- Reference this class context before building anything. Do not produce a generic Primary 4 resource.
- When building an interactive, build in one moment where a student has to predict before they see the answer.
- When writing for parents, assume they are busy and will read on a phone. Short paragraphs, no bullet points.

## What to avoid

- Do not assume prior knowledge beyond what is listed above.
- Do not use the word "photosynthesis" without immediately following it with what it means.
- Do not produce an activity that requires printing. This class is paperless this term.
